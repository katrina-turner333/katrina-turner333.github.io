EQ and IQ

What is Emotional Intelligence?

Having the ability to understand your emotions and to have control over them, instead of having feeling control you. Ability to understand other people’s emotions. Ability to think before you act and to control yourself. Ability to build and maintain relationships.

How is it different to IQ?

IQ is a statistical number that indicates a person’s logical and reasoning ability compared to the statistical norm. EQ is based on understanding emotions.

Why is it important?

Having a good EQ can support the advancement towards academic and professional success, improve relationships, and boost communication skills. The value of EQ is immense; developing emotional intelligence encourages many positive traits, from resilience to communication, motivation to stress management, all of which can be seen as conducive to effectively achieving personal, physical and occupational health, and success.
